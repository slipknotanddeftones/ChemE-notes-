reboilers
simulink

1. internal re-boiler


U-tubes are added at the bottom of the column for heating the fluid. it has 2 disadvantages-

  1. distillation columns are not wide enough to accommodate the tubes
  2. difficult to support the end of bundle but this can usually be accomplished

2. kettle reboilers

  1. k type TEMA shell
  2. boiling takes place on the shell side
  3. thermosiphon action- gravity feed in this case
  4. fluid is sometimes pumped
  5. bottoms are taken from the overflow from the weir in the reboiler
  6. dia: 2-10 ft, height- 8-40 ft
  7. minimum vapour space off 0.25 m
  8. tube bundle- semi circular
  9. liq level is controlled by a weir
  10. top of the weir is between 2-6 inches
  11. U tubes, straight tubes with floating head for fouling liqs (cleaning)
  12. main flow is naturally in direction normal to the bundle axis, so plates don't affect much
  13. exit nozzle is equipped with means to minimize the entrainment of liquids (pad demister)

problems
collection of scale and other solid materials in the tube bundle region, leading to fouling, control is necessary by purging.

3. vertical thermosiphon (very important)

  1. T.E.M.A E type shell with single tube side-pass
  2. tube side circuit by pipe connecting the reboiler with the bottom of the distillation column
  3. boiling takes place in the tubes heating is supplied by process stream
  4. liq level in the distillation column is maintained well above the bottom tube plate and maintained level with the top tube plate
  5. flow through reboiler is achieved by natural circulation because density is greater at the bottom of the tubes as compared to the liq-vap mixture in the ex-changer tubes
  6. tube dimensions: L- 8-16 ft, less than 1.8 m, uneconomical design
  7. outlet has different design types, side outlet being the most common. flow area in the outlet nozzle is same as that in the tubes to avoid instability. inlet nozzle is usually at the bottom or side. diameter of inlet is less than outlet since inlet has only liquid phase entering.
  8. vertical thermosiphons can be operated at a wide ranges of pressure. however for pressures lower than 0.2 bar there might be nucleate boiling because of relatively larger amount of vapor produced. since at lower pressures the sensible heat required also decreases. similar for high pressure operations (above 0.9 ), it is easy to exceed the critical heat flux and the phase density difference that drives the circulation is small.
  9. re-circulation ratio
    where is the quality of mixture at the reboiler exit.

problems

  • may be sub-cooling due to the hydro-static head at the bottom of the tubes in which saturation is approached, because the liquid liquid temperature increases. for atmospheric conditions the sub-cooling length is usually 1/10th of the tube length but may be larger for vacuums.
