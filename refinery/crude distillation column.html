crude distillation column

[17 jan], [18 jan]
CDU furnace image

  • higher reflux, better mixing.

  • heating- overhead heat is used to condense reflux stream. to reach VLE. but it is multicomponent distillation.

  • crude oil (heavy ends at 360C, components will also boil at 370C)

  • increases traffic for no reason. Overhead system is only for lighter ends, for middle ends we need a reflux .

  • reflux allows to take away the sensible heat (middle part "pump around"). pump arounds controlling liq and electron traffic and helps in better heat transfer. it is not feasible to operate the distillation optimally without pump around.

    Info

    oleophillic- attracted to oil
    oleophobic- rejects oil

  • heating the crude, then desalting, then heating again

  • no condenstion is required at the top. called sour water, because contains sulphur. temperature is fixed by calculating the composition of gas stream (condensation temp is kept 15C more than the calculated temp)

  • heater

  • uses radiation. most gases use the radiation zone and rest heat is tranfered by convection.

  • to prevent sulphur trioxide condensation, the temp is decided in the heater. (around 140-150C)

  • heat recovery by the distillation unit. how well the column is sized up determines the efficiency of heat recovery.

  • losses- heater, heat exchangers, condenser, cooling tower.

  • NPSH calculations, centrifugal pumps in series and parallel.

flash zone

  • temp limited by advent of cracking.
  • pressure set by setting reflux drum pressure.
  • adds washing action- some coke that gets formed and deposits in the column gets washed out.
  • over flash- 3-5% of liq volume.

overcut and gap

  • naphtha frac- 30-203C

  • 155-197 overlap for pump around

  • 5% boling point of HF, 95% boiling point of LF: difference gives ASTM gap

  • ideally, gap should be 0.

  • HF- higher frac, LF- lower frac
