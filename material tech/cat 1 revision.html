eutectoid reaction of steel

so steel has majorly three types of phases.

  • alpha ferrite is BCC and contains 0.02 %C.
  • austentite is FCC and contains about 2% C.
  • cementite is BCC and contains about 6.67% C

eutectoid means that one solid state transforms into 2 solid phases.

so for Fe-C or steel, a eutectoid reaction occurs at 727 ℃. in this reaction, forms and . in this reaction, C and Fe both diffuse in and out to give rise to the final phases.

![[]]

NOTE that pearlite is a microstructure but ferrite and austentite are phases.
also pearlite is strong due to dispersion strengthening.

dispersion strengthening is adding of a small amount of a different phase to help with inference and leading to strengthening

Hypo, hyper and eutectoid alloys

0.77% C is eutectoid, less than that is hypo and more is hyper.

hypoeutectoid

  • major constitutent is ferrite. (or procontsituent)
  • if we heat hypo steel above 750⁰C, only austentite remains in the microstructure.
  • when u cool hypo eutectoid austentite, begins to form at the grain boundaries. at 727 ⁰C, the eutectoid reaction happens for remaining austentite. so this means the final steel is a mixture of microconstituents of pearlite and ferrite.
  • island of pearlite, surrounded by primary ferrite.
  • this structure is strong, due to pearlite and ductile due to ferrite.

hypereutectoid

  • primary phase is cementite.
  • if one stzrts with austentite, and starts cooling it, at ~820 ⁰C, cementite begins to form. at the eutectoid temp, remaining austentite starts to form and
  • so end product is hard, brittle cementite in islands of pearlite.

different phases/micro constituents and associated property

  1. pearlite- strong,
  2. ferrite- relatively soft,
  3. cementite- brittle
